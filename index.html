About

I’m not just an engineering student — I’m a system thinker, a builder of thoughtful tools, and someone who believes that software should feel as intuitive as it is intelligent. I'm Shabdansh Prajapati, a student at Thakur College of Engineering, deeply curious about how ideas become systems — and how those systems can help people. Whether it's a romantic note-sharing app, a structured file-sharing platform, or a minimalist attendance tracker, every project I build carries a story and a sense of purpose. I value clarity, both in code and in life. My learning style is slow, deep, and intentional. I document everything, organize obsessively (Notion is my second brain), and build with emotion. I enjoy creating things that are clean, calming, and meaningful — apps that feel quietly powerful. I'm currently learning full-stack development with Firebase, pushing mini-projects to GitHub, and reflecting on the deeper side of AI through courses like Harvard’s CS50P and Elements of AI. I treat each line of code not just as logic, but as a piece of who I am becoming. If you’re looking for a developer who blends technical insight with emotional creativity — welcome to my space.
